# Soft Machine Drift
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Project ID:** AG09
**Library:** p5.js

## Concept
A field of biomorphic capsule-forms emerge and dissolve in overlapping clusters, their soft pill-shaped bodies constructed from bezier curves with noise-displaced edges. The forms breathe, rotate slowly, and occasionally split or merge, creating a tactile landscape of translucent color masses that recall pharmaceutical tablets, organic tissue, and modernist textile patterns. The piece explores tension between geometric intention and organic distortion.

## Palette
#f4f1e8 (pale cream background — quiet substrate), #4a7c8e (dusty teal — primary mass), #d94e72 (warm pink — secondary mass), #8b5a7d (muted plum — tertiary mass), #c9a84e (ochre yellow — rare accent), #2d5f3f (deep forest green — shadow layer), #e87451 (coral orange — punctuation)

## Composition
The canvas operates on a radial organization rather than grid. Phase 1: forms spawn along canvas perimeter (within 100px of edges), distributed in 8 cardinal/ordinal compass points, creating ring formation with hollow center spanning 400px diameter. Phase 2: forms spiral inward along logarithmic paths, collapsing into central vortex occupying 320px diameter circle, creating dense layered core with 12-18 capsules stacked. Phase 3: explosive radial ejection — forms shoot outward along their original entry vectors but reversed, traveling to opposite edge positions, creating X-pattern crossings through center. Throughout, forms maintain variable separation: 120-200px during ring phase, 15-40px during vortex compression, 180-300px during ejection. Z-layering: 4 depth planes, innermost plane rotates counterclockwise at 0.012 rad/frame, outer planes progressively slower (0.008, 0.005, 0.002 rad/frame).

## Elements
PRIMARY: Capsule-bodies constructed via beginShape()/vertex()/bezierVertex()/endShape(CLOSE). Each capsule has 2 semicircular ends connected by parallel bezier curves. Base dimensions 80-180px length, 40-90px width. Edge displacement: Perlin noise sampled at 20-30 points around perimeter, offset 3-12px perpendicular to surface normal, noise frequency 0.08, evolves over time at 0.001/frame. Count: 24-32 capsules. SECONDARY: Trail ghosts — previous positions of capsules rendered at 8% opacity, creates motion blur echo. Each capsule leaves 8-15 ghost frames. TERTIARY: Micro-granules — tiny bezier blobs (6-12px) that cluster at capsule intersections, count 120-200, constructed from 6-vertex irregular polygons with corner rounding via quadraticVertex(). TEXTURE LAYER: Background noise texture from 4000 overlapping translucent ellipses (2-8px) at 3% opacity, regenerates every 480 frames.

## Animation
PHASE 1 (0-28s / 0-1680 frames): Capsules materialize along canvas perimeter at 8 spawn zones (N, NE, E, SE, S, SW, W, NW), 3-4 capsules per zone appearing simultaneously in bursts every 180-240 frames. Upon spawn, capsules orbit their spawn point in 220px radius circles at 1.2-1.9px/frame tangential velocity, creating localized circulation currents. Each capsule has independent orbital direction (50% clockwise, 50% counter). Rotation: 0.006-0.011 radians/frame per capsule. Micro-granules spawn at 5/frame, magnetically snap to nearest capsule edge within 80px, forming dynamic corona. PHASE 2 (28-72s / 1680-4320 frames): Spiral collapse initiated. Each capsule's orbital path begins tightening — radius decreases at 0.8px/frame while angular velocity increases exponentially (multiply by 1.008 each frame, capping at 0.028 rad/frame). Movement traces logarithmic spiral toward canvas center: r(θ) = 220 * e^(-0.004θ). Transit duration: 1200 frames to reach central vortex. During compression (frames 2400-3600), capsules begin figure-8 weaving patterns within 320px central zone, crossing paths create momentary occlusion flickers. Rotation: accelerates to 0.021 radians/frame, then reverses direction every 280 frames creating oscillating spin. Color blending: capsules within 45px begin 240-frame color morph, blending toward average hue. Micro-granules form dense swarm cloud (opacity increases to 18%), rotating around vortex center at 0.035 rad/frame. PHASE 3 (72s-120s / 4320-7200 frames): Explosive ejection sequence. At frame 4320, all capsules simultaneously receive velocity vectors pointing radially outward from canvas center, initial speed 5.8px/frame. Velocity governed by easeOutQuint, decelerating to 0.9px/frame by frame 5400. Capsules travel to positions on opposite canvas edge from origin (180° rotation around center), creating dramatic crossover choreography through center zone (frames 4320-4800). Capsules passing through center (within 160px) trigger cascade splits: 40% split probability, division produces 2 children at 70% parent size, children receive perpendicular velocity offset ±2.4px/frame. Rotation: decelerates to 0.003 radians/frame but adds wobble (sin(frameCount * 0.08) * 0.02 radians). Micro-granules detach from capsules at frame 5000, drift toward canvas corners at 0.7px/frame, fade to 0 opacity over 900 frames. Trail ghosts extend to 28 frames, creating dramatic speed streaks. At frame 6900, all elements begin 300-frame fade to prepare restart.

## Transformation
CAPSULE LIFECYCLE: Birth (frames 0-120): scale from 0.2 to 1.0 using easeOutBack (overshoot to 1.22 at frame 85, settle to 1.0 by frame 120). Opacity 0 to 0.8 with easeInQuad curve. Edge noise displacement starts at maximum (12px), decreases to base 3-12px range by frame 120 (inverted entrance). OSCILLATION: All capsules pulse using desynchronized noise-driven rhythm: scale = 1.0 + noise(id * 100 + frameCount * 0.015) * 0.12, creates organic breathing variations between 0.88-1.12 scale, each capsule on unique 6-8 second cycle. NOISE EVOLUTION: Edge displacement noise offset increments by 0.0015/frame (50% faster than previous version), causing more active morphing — full shape transformation cycle is 667 frames (11.1 seconds). COLOR SHIFT: Each capsule's hue shifts ±12° over 600 frame cycles using sawtooth wave (creates directional color drift rather than oscillation). SPLIT TRANSFORMATION (Phase 3): Parent capsule scales 1.0 to 0.3 over 60 frames (easeInQuint), rotates 360° during collapse (0.105 rad/frame). Two children emerge from parent center offset by ±30px perpendicular to parent velocity vector, scaling 0 to 0.7 over 75 frames with 15-frame stagger, inherit parent momentum + perpendicular component. VORTEX COMPRESSION (Phase 2): During frames 2400-3600, capsules in central zone undergo rhythmic squash-stretch: length oscillates ±15% at 0.04 Hz (period = 150 frames), width ±10% at 0.06 Hz (period = 100 frames), offsets create organic writhing.

## Rhythm
MACRO: Gathering ring (28s) → spiraling vortex (44s) → explosive scatter (48s) = 120-second cycle. Phase transitions are abrupt: Phase 1→2 triggers simultaneously at frame 1680 (all capsules begin spiral at once). Phase 2→3 triggers with synchronized ejection burst at frame 4320 (dramatic energy release). This version emphasizes punctuated equilibrium rather than gradual drift. MICRO: Individual capsule movement has irregular pulse (noise-driven 6-8s breathing) rather than synchronized rhythm, creating polyrhythmic visual texture. Rotation includes wobble oscillation at 0.79-second frequency (60 frames), adding jitter to otherwise smooth spin. Micro-granules update position every 2 frames (not every frame), creating subtle stutter effect against capsule smoothness. Trail ghosts have variable opacity (8-18% based on capsule velocity), making faster capsules more visually prominent. Split events in Phase 3 occur in rapid cascade (12-18 splits compressed into 320-frame window, frames 4400-4720), creating concentrated transformation burst rather than distributed punctuation. Vortex phase includes 150-frame period oscillations (squash-stretch), establishing medium-frequency visual beat.

## Interaction (mouse)
MOUSE POSITION: Mouse X (0 to width) controls vortex center horizontal offset: left edge = -180px from canvas center, right edge = +180px from canvas center, mapped with easeInOutCubic, allowing user to steer the spiral destination during Phase 2. Mouse Y (0 to height) controls global time dilation: top = 0.6x animation speed (slow motion), center = 1.0x, bottom = 1.8x animation speed (fast forward), affects frameCount increment and all time-based calculations, linear mapping. MOUSE PRESS: Click injects angular momentum into system. All capsules within 400px of click receive tangential velocity boost perpendicular to radius from click point, magnitude 3.2px/frame with inverse linear falloff (velocity = 3.2 * (1 - dist/400)). Effect persists for 180 frames, causes local spiral perturbations. Click also spawns 40 micro-granules at click position that orbit click point for 240 frames at 2.8px/frame tangential velocity, then dissipate. MOUSE DRAG: While dragging, capsules within 280px of cursor experience repulsion force: velocity away from cursor at 2.1px/frame, inverse square falloff (force = 2.1 * (1 - dist/280)^2), creates local void that follows cursor, useful for clearing space or disrupting vortex. PASSIVE FALLBACK: When mouse hasn't moved for 90 frames, system locks to autonomous mode — vortex center returns to canvas center over 180 frames (easeInOutQuad), time dilation returns to 1.0x, no momentum injection or repulsion active.
