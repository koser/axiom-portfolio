# Interference Pattern
**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Library:** canvas

## Concept
A system of vertical ellipsoids engaged in continuous physical negotiation—pushing, pulling, and influencing each other's positions through proximity-based forces. Forms compress when nearby neighbors encroach, stretch when pulled by distant attractions, and oscillate through states of tension and release. The work explores emergent rhythm through interaction rather than predetermined pattern, questioning whether visual harmony arises from external order or internal relationships.

## Palette
Saturated primaries (yellow, blue, coral red) against deep green and cream on powder blue ground. Colours maintain distinct boundaries without blending—flat, assertive, with no gradients or transparency to preserve the tension between overlapping forms.

## Interaction
Ellipsoids autonomously animate through a spring-physics system where each form experiences attraction and repulsion forces from its neighbors. Close proximity triggers compression (forms narrow and elongate), while separation creates elastic pull. The system seeks but never settles into equilibrium—continuous micro-adjustments create living rhythm. Mouse position acts as a gravitational well, subtly biasing the entire field without direct control, allowing observation of the system's self-organizing behavior.
