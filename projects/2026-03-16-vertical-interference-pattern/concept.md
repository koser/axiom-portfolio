# Vertical Interference Pattern
**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Project ID:** AG23
**Library:** p5.js

## Concept
A study in aggressive visual rhythm disruption through scanning interference. The piece channels the moodboard's harsh vertical striping and glitch-like fragmentation into an autonomous system where sharp vertical bands are progressively torn apart by diagonal scanning waves. This is not about harmony — it's about controlled visual violence, signal degradation, and the moment just before resolution collapses into noise.

## Palette
#0a0a0a (void — deep background, infinite depth), #ffffff (primary — sharp vertical bands, pure contrast), #1a1a1a (secondary — interference shadow, subtle mass between bands), #f2f2f2 (accent — damaged edges, half-failed signal)

## Composition
The canvas is divided into 18-22 vertical bands (width varies 20-35px) spanning full height, alternating #ffffff and #0a0a0a with 1-2px gaps of #1a1a1a between them. Initial state: perfect vertical regularity, centered layout with 40px margins. Phase 1: bands begin wobbling at their top anchors. Phase 2: diagonal interference waves (60-80 degrees from horizontal) sweep from bottom-left to top-right every 3.5 seconds, fragmenting bands into displaced segments. Phase 3: bands lose vertical integrity entirely, becoming staggered shards with 5-15px horizontal displacement, some segments missing entirely (dropout zones). Negative space becomes an active compositional force — the gaps are as important as the marks.

## Elements
PRIMARY: Vertical bands constructed as custom quad chains using beginShape(QUADS). Each band is subdivided into 40-60 segments vertically. Each segment is a quad with vertex positions that can be displaced independently. Construction: for each segment, define 4 vertices (top-left, top-right, bottom-right, bottom-left). Apply noise-based displacement to x-positions using noise(segmentIndex * 0.1, frameCount * 0.003) * displacementAmplitude (starts at 0, ramps to 8-15px). SECONDARY: Diagonal scan lines — translucent (#ffffff at 15-25% opacity) bezier curves, 3-5px thick, sweeping at 70-degree angle. Each scan line is constructed with bezierVertex() creating slight S-curve wobble. 6-8 scan lines active simultaneously, spaced 80-120px apart, moving at 4.2px/frame. TERTIARY: Dropout artifacts — rectangular voids (5-12px wide, 15-40px tall) that appear in band segments after scan waves pass. These are rendered as #0a0a0a rects with 1px #1a1a1a borders, positioned randomly within damaged segments. 15-30 dropouts active at peak phase. TEXTURE: Subtle vertical grain overlay using nested loop of 1px lines with random(0.7, 1.0) opacity, density 1 line per 3px horizontally, full height.

## Animation
Phase 1 (0-12s): Bands establish then destabilize. First 3s: static perfect stripes. 3-7s: introduce top-anchor wobble using sin(frameCount * 0.02 + bandIndex * 0.3) * 2px horizontal displacement, affects only top 15% of band height. 7-12s: wobble amplitude increases to 5px, propagates down to 40% of band height with 0.8s delay (exponential easing). Phase 2 (12-45s): Scanning interference waves. Every 3.5s, spawn a diagonal scan wave at bottom-left (-100, height + 100). Scan moves at 4.2px/frame along vector (cos(70deg) * 4.2, sin(70deg) * -4.2). As scan intersects each band segment (collision detected via line-rect intersection), that segment's displacement amplitude jumps from current to 8-15px over 8 frames (cubic ease-out), then slowly decays back to 3-6px over 120 frames. Scan lines themselves fade in over 15 frames (0 to 25% opacity), hold, fade out over 20 frames after leaving viewport. Phase 3 (45s+): Structural collapse and partial recovery. Global displacement baseline increases to 6-10px. Every 5-7s, random band segments (8-15 per event) experience dropout: segment opacity drops to 0 over 12 frames, stays invisible for 60-180 frames, then flickers back (3 frame on/off cycles) before returning to full opacity over 30 frames. Simultaneously, horizontal offset accumulates: segments drift horizontally using accumulated noise(segmentIndex * 0.05, millis() * 0.0001) * 12px, creating permanent stagger. Every 12s, system partially resets: 30% of segments snap back toward vertical alignment over 90 frames (exponential ease-in-out), while others remain damaged.

## Transformation
Each band segment undergoes continuous transformation via layered noise functions. Primary displacement: noise(segmentY * 0.08, time * 0.003) maps to -15 to +15px horizontal offset. Secondary wobble: sin(time * 0.04 + segmentIndex) * 2.5px adds rhythmic breathing. When scan wave hits, displacement multiplier jumps from 1.0 to 2.8 over 8 frames using pow(t, 0.3) easing curve, decays back to 1.2 over 120 frames using exponential decay (multiplier *= 0.985 per frame). Dropout transformations: opacity follows sequence [1.0 → 0.0 over 12 frames] → [hold 0.0 for 60-180 frames] → [flicker: 1.0/0.0 alternating every 3 frames, 5 cycles] → [0.0 → 1.0 over 30 frames with cubic ease]. Scale remains constant at 1.0 — all transformation is positional/opacity. Vertical grain overlay animates via slow vertical scroll: offset increases at 0.15px/frame with wraparound.

## Rhythm
Macro rhythm: 3.5-second scan wave cycle creates primary pulse. 12-second collapse/recovery super-cycle governs global state shifts. Micro rhythm: individual segments operate on 4-6 second noise cycles (breathe in/out based on noise frequency 0.003), punctuated by sudden 8-frame impact spikes when scans hit. Dropout events create syncopation — unpredictable gaps in the visual field every 5-7 seconds. The piece alternates between rigid vertical order (3-7s holds) and chaotic displacement storms (scan impacts + dropouts). Silence (perfect vertical stripes) occurs only in first 3 seconds and briefly (0.8-1.2s) after major reset events at 45s, 90s marks. Overall pacing: aggressive but not frantic — violence through precision, not speed.

## Interaction (passive)
Fully autonomous system driven by frameCount and millis() for temporal control, multi-octave Perlin noise fields for organic displacement (noise(x, y, t) sampled at 3 frequencies: 0.003 for slow drift, 0.015 for medium wobble, 0.08 for fine texture), and scheduled event system for scan wave spawning (every 210 frames = 3.5s at 60fps) and dropout triggers (random intervals between 300-420 frames). Scan wave spawning uses modulo: if (frameCount % 210 === 0) spawn new scan. Dropout events use probabilistic triggering: each frame, each segment has 0.0008 probability of entering dropout state, capped at 30 simultaneous dropouts. Reset events fire at fixed intervals (frameCount === 2700, 5400, etc.) affecting random 30% subset of segments selected via random seed reset.
