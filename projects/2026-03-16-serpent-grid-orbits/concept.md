# Serpent Grid Orbits
**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Project ID:** AG19
**Library:** p5.js

## Concept
Four distinct quadrants in a 2x2 grid, each containing a 5x5 arrangement of gray circles that act as gravitational wells. A black line serpent with finite length (800px) continuously grows from its head while retracting from its tail, maintaining constant total length. Each quadrant features a different hunting pattern: top-left uses tight spiral wrapping, top-right employs angular zigzag weaving, bottom-left creates flowing S-curve embraces, bottom-right traces geometric hexagonal captures. The line is drawn as a smooth catmull-rom spline through dynamically updated control points, creating organic curves as it seeks the nearest unwrapped circle. Circles pulse with magnetic attraction fields (15-25px radius) that pull the line's growth direction, and neighboring circles within each quadrant push/pull against each other creating subtle oscillating drift (±3-8px amplitude, 4-7 second periods, phase-shifted). When the serpent wraps a circle (passing within 12px), that circle blooms to 1.4x size over 0.6 seconds then slowly deflates. The line never stops evolving — as the head seeks new circles, the tail retracts from old territory, creating perpetual organic motion that balances exploration and constraint.

## Palette
#f8f6f2 (canvas background - warm paper white), #0d0d0d (serpent line - ink black with 3px stroke weight), #c9c9c9 (inactive circles - neutral mid gray), #1a1a1a (serpent shadow pass - offset 2px for depth), #e5e5e5 (circle bloom state - soft highlight), #7a7a7a (circle core during wrap - darker inner fill)

## Composition
Canvas divided into strict 2x2 grid with 8px separator gutters. Each quadrant: 480x480px containing a 5x5 circle grid with 85px spacing, circles at 18px base radius. Top-left quadrant origin at (8, 8), top-right at (496, 8), bottom-left at (8, 496), bottom-right at (496, 8). Each serpent spawns at its quadrant center and begins hunting immediately. The spatial structure remains fixed but circles drift within ±8px of their grid positions. Serpent paths create dense central tangles that gradually expand outward seeking perimeter circles before collapsing inward again.

## Elements
Circles: 100 total (25 per quadrant), base radius 18px, stroke #c9c9c9 at 2.5px weight, no fill when inactive. Attraction field invisible but active at 15-25px beyond circle edge. Circles constructed as simple geometric circles with dual-layer rendering during bloom (outer stroke + inner fill). Serpents: 4 total (one per quadrant), each consisting of 40-60 dynamically positioned control points feeding into catmull-rom spline renderer with alpha 0.5 smoothing. Line thickness 3px, smooth endcaps. Each control point has velocity vector (1.2-2.8 px/frame magnitude) pointing toward nearest unwrapped target circle, modulated by attraction field strength. Tail retracts at same speed as head advances, removing oldest control points. Shadow layer: duplicate serpent path offset by (2, 2)px rendered at 15% opacity underneath main line.

## Animation
PHASE 1 (0-45 seconds): Initial exploration. All four serpents spawn at quadrant centers and begin seeking nearest circles. Top-left serpent uses spiral algorithm, wrapping circles in concentric rings from center outward at 2.1px/frame head speed. Top-right uses corner-prioritizing zigzag at 2.4px/frame. Bottom-left employs fluid S-curve flow at 1.8px/frame with high bezier smoothing. Bottom-right traces hexagonal lattice paths at 2.2px/frame with 60-degree angle preferences. Circles pulse at 0.8-1.2 second intervals with ±2px radius variation. PHASE 2 (45-110 seconds): Territorial density. Serpents have wrapped 60-80% of their circles, creating dense overlapping paths. Circle drift accelerates to ±8px amplitude at 5-second periods. Wrapped circles maintain 1.15x enlarged state. Serpent heads slow to 1.4-1.9px/frame as they navigate congested territories. Attraction fields strengthen to 28px radius, creating stronger pulls. PHASE 3 (110+ seconds): Perpetual reorganization. Serpents abandon wrapped circles as tails retract, allowing circles to deflate over 2.4 seconds back to base size. Heads seek newly available circles, creating migration patterns: top-left collapses to center then re-expands, top-right sweeps left-right scanning, bottom-left creates figure-8 patterns, bottom-right spirals counter-clockwise. Circle pulse becomes synchronized within quadrants (8-circle groups pulsing together) at 2.1-second intervals. Head speed varies 1.6-2.6px/frame based on local circle density.

## Transformation
Circle drift transforms through perlin noise fields (noise scale 0.008, separate per quadrant) applied to x/y offsets from grid positions, creating organic breathing motion. Each circle samples noise at (gridX * 0.008 + time * 0.0003, gridY * 0.008) and applies result * 8px as position offset. Push/pull between neighboring circles (within 120px distance) calculated as inverse-square repulsion with 0.4 strength coefficient, adding ±1-3px directional offset. Serpent control points transform via target-seeking vectors: each point calculates angle to nearest unwrapped circle center, modulated by attraction field strength (1.0 at field edge, 2.5 at circle surface) and random turbulence (±12 degrees). When point reaches within 12px of target, that circle marked as wrapped and new target acquired. Tail retraction simply removes oldest control point each frame. Spline tension dynamically adjusts: top-left uses 0.6 (tight curves), top-right 0.3 (angular), bottom-left 0.8 (flowing), bottom-right 0.4 (geometric).

## Rhythm
Each quadrant operates on distinct temporal signature. Top-left: 2.1-second circle pulse cycle, serpent completes full spiral wrap in 32-38 seconds before tail catches up. Top-right: 1.7-second pulse, zigzag completes horizontal scan in 28-34 seconds. Bottom-left: 2.6-second pulse, S-curve flow creates 42-48 second cycles. Bottom-right: 1.9-second pulse, hexagonal trace completes in 35-41 seconds. Global rhythm: every 8 seconds, all quadrants synchronize for single unified pulse. Circle size oscillations range 18-25.2px (base to bloom) with 0.6-second attack, 2.4-second decay. Serpent speed modulates at 0.3 Hz creating gentle acceleration/deceleration waves. Noise field time parameter advances at 0.0003 per frame (assuming 60fps) creating slow continental drift of circle positions over 4-5 minute cycles.
