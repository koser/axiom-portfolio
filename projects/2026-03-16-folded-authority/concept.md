# Folded Authority
**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Project ID:** AG17
**Library:** p5.js

## Concept
A study in the tension between rigid geometric cuts and soft, billowing forms. Large, irregular polygonal shapes with subtly curved edges fold and unfold in deliberate, architectural movements, their surfaces disrupted by thin, diagonal slashes that reveal darkness beneath. The piece explores the visual language of the moodboard's bold, assertive shapes and the violence of the diagonal incision — a composition about interruption, layering, and the slow breathing of monumental forms.

## Palette
#0d0d0d (void — deep background and revealed negative space), #f7f5f0 (mass — primary fill for the folded shapes), #1a1a1a (shadow — subtle edge darkening and depth cues), #0a0a0a (cut — the thin diagonal wounds)

## Composition
The canvas is divided by 3-5 large, irregular polygonal masses that occupy 60-80% of the viewport. These shapes cluster in the center-left and upper-right quadrants, leaving breathing room in the lower-left. Each mass is constructed from 6-12 vertices with bezier-smoothed edges. A thin diagonal slash (2-4px width, rotated -15 to -25 degrees) cuts through each mass, revealing the void beneath. Over time, the masses shift position by 80-150px over 20-30 second intervals, creating new overlaps and negative space configurations. The composition breathes between dense clustering (Phase 1) and sparse distribution (Phase 3).

## Elements
PRIMARY MASSES: 3-5 large irregular polygons built with beginShape()/vertex()/bezierVertex()/endShape(CLOSE). Each mass has 6-12 anchor points positioned using Perlin noise (noise scale 0.003) to create organic asymmetry. Edge curves use bezierVertex() with control points offset by 40-80px from anchors, creating soft billowing edges. Size range: 280-550px across longest axis. Each mass has a subtle noise-based breathing effect on vertices (±3-8px displacement at 0.02Hz). DIAGONAL CUTS: Thin rectangular voids (2-4px width, 400-900px length) that pierce each mass at -15 to -25 degree angles. These are clipping masks that reveal the background void. EDGE SHADOWS: Semi-transparent (#1a1a1a at 15-30% opacity) offset duplicates of each mass, positioned 4-8px down and right, creating subtle depth. NOISE TEXTURE: A layer of 8000-12000 tiny semi-transparent circles (0.5-1.5px diameter, #0d0d0d at 3-8% opacity) scattered across the entire canvas, creating a subtle grain reminiscent of paper texture.

## Animation
PHASE 1 (0-25s): Masses slowly breathe in place. Each vertex oscillates with Perlin noise (noise(xoff + frameCount * 0.001)) creating organic edge wobble at ±3-8px amplitude. The entire mass rotates around its centroid at 0.08-0.15 degrees/frame (one full rotation every 40-75 seconds). Diagonal cuts remain fixed relative to each mass, moving with it. Edge shadows lag behind parent mass by 0.3-0.5 seconds (using lerp with factor 0.08), creating a subtle trailing depth effect. PHASE 2 (25-80s): Masses begin lateral migration. Each mass selects a new target position (100-180px away from current) and glides toward it using exponential easing (lerp factor 0.015, creating slow acceleration then deceleration over 18-25 seconds). As masses move, their rotation speed increases to 0.2-0.35 degrees/frame. When two masses approach within 60px, they subtly repel each other (velocity adjustment of 0.8-1.5px/frame perpendicular to approach vector). Diagonal cuts start to slowly rotate independently of their parent masses at ±0.05 degrees/frame, creating visual tension. PHASE 3 (80s+): Masses enter a folding state. Over 12-18 seconds, each mass's vertices begin to collapse toward the centroid (lerp factor 0.008-0.012), shrinking the shape to 40-60% original size. Simultaneously, new vertices are inserted at bezier control points, causing the shape to refold into a more complex 12-18 point polygon. The diagonal cut width pulses (2px to 7px over 4-second sinusoidal cycle). Once fully folded, masses expand back to 100-120% original size over 8-12 seconds (exponential easing), then return to Phase 1 behaviour. Phase transitions are staggered by 3-7 seconds per mass to avoid synchronization.

## Transformation
Each mass undergoes a continuous breathing transformation: vertices displace using layered Perlin noise at three frequencies (0.001Hz for slow drift, 0.008Hz for breathing, 0.02Hz for subtle jitter) with combined amplitude 3-8px. Over 60-90 second intervals, masses execute a fold cycle: vertices lerp toward centroid (0.012/frame) for 720-1080 frames, hold compressed state for 180-300 frames, then expand (0.018/frame) for 480-720 frames. During compression, opacity fades from 100% to 92% (linear). Diagonal cuts pulse width using sin(frameCount * 0.008) * 2.5 + 3.5, creating 2-6px oscillation over 4-second cycles. Edge shadows maintain constant 6px offset but opacity oscillates 15-30% using sin(frameCount * 0.005).

## Rhythm
MACRO RHYTHM: The piece operates on overlapping 60-90 second breath cycles per mass, intentionally desynchronized. This creates a slow, tectonic pacing where the entire composition is always in subtle motion but never chaotic. Every 80-100 seconds, one mass completes a full fold/unfold cycle, punctuating the otherwise continuous drift. MICRO RHYTHM: Individual vertex wobble occurs at 0.02Hz (50-second period), creating imperceptible organic motion. Rotation is constant at 0.08-0.35 degrees/frame depending on phase. Cut rotation is glacial at 0.05 degrees/frame (120 seconds per full rotation). The stagger between mass phase transitions is 3-7 seconds, ensuring visual events cascade rather than synchronize. Overall feel: monumental, deliberate, authoritative — like watching continental plates shift.

## Interaction (mouse)
MOUSE X POSITION: Controls the angle of all diagonal cuts. Mapped from 0 degrees (left edge) to -45 degrees (right edge) using map(mouseX, 0, width, 0, -PI/4). All cuts respond with lerp smoothing (factor 0.06) creating a 1-second lag. MOUSE Y POSITION: Controls the global rotation speed multiplier for all masses. Mapped from 0.5x (top edge) to 2.5x (bottom edge) using map(mouseY, 0, height, 0.5, 2.5). When mouse is in top 20% of screen, masses freeze rotation entirely (multiplier lerps to 0 over 1.2 seconds). MOUSE CLICK: Triggers an immediate fold event for the mass nearest to cursor (within 300px radius). The targeted mass rapidly collapses to 30% size over 45 frames (exponential easing), holds for 30 frames, then explodes back to 130% size over 60 frames before settling to 100% over 90 frames (elastic easing with overshoot). Click also spawns 8-15 small temporary polygonal fragments (15-40px, 4-7 vertices each) at click position that drift outward at 1.5-4px/frame with rotation 2-8 degrees/frame, fading from 80% to 0% opacity over 180-240 frames. PASSIVE FALLBACK: When mouse is stationary for >5 seconds or outside canvas, cuts return to -20 degree default (lerp 0.03) and rotation multiplier returns to 1.0 (lerp 0.02). All autonomous behaviours continue unaffected.
