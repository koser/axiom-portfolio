# Tessellated Flux Membrane
**Date:** 2026-03-17
**Project ID:** AG25
**Library:** p5.js

## Concept
A monochromatic field of organic, pattern-filled cellular forms that continuously subdivide, merge, and exchange textures. The piece explores the tension between rigid geometric patterning (halftone, chevron, grid) and fluid biomorphic boundaries—wobbling membranes containing contradictory interior worlds. Each cell is a micro-composition breathing against its neighbors, creating a shifting landscape of black, white, and gray territories in constant territorial renegotiation.

## Palette
#EBEBEB (background — pale neutral field), #0A0A0A (primary — deep black forms and patterns), #7A7A7A (secondary — mid-gray transitions and halftone masses), #FFFFFF (accent — white negative spaces and pattern inversions)

## Composition
The canvas is divided into 8-12 large biomorphic cells (blob-like territories) that never touch edges—always separated by the pale background acting as negative space rivers. Cells cluster more densely in the center-left and bottom-right quadrants initially, leaving the top-right sparse. Over 45 seconds, cells migrate diagonally (bottom-left to top-right), redistributing weight. By Phase 3, the composition inverts: dense top-right, sparse bottom-left. Cells range from 120px to 380px in their longest axis. Background rivers widen and narrow (40px to 120px) as cells breathe.

## Elements
PRIMARY: Textured Biomorphic Cells — constructed using curveVertex() with 12-18 control points, edges displaced by Perlin noise (noise scale 0.008, amplitude 8-25px per vertex, evolving at 0.002/frame). Each cell's interior is NOT a solid fill but a dense procedural texture: either (A) halftone dots (5-12px diameter circles in tight grid, 60-80% coverage), (B) chevron/wave patterns (3-6px stroke weight, angled 30-150°, spacing 8-15px), (C) dense crosshatch (overlapping lines at 45° and 135°, 2px stroke, 6px spacing), or (D) mesh/net texture (overlapping bezier curves creating irregular lattice, 1-3px stroke). Texture assignment is persistent per cell. Count: 8-12 cells. SECONDARY: Boundary Distortion Tendrils — thin (1-2px), thread-like bezier curves that occasionally sprout from cell edges when two cells approach within 30px, creating temporary bridge-like connections. 3-8 tendrils active at any moment, each lasting 90-180 frames before dissolving. TERTIARY: Pattern Migration Particles — when cells overlap or come within 15px, their interior patterns 'leak' as small (3-6px) fragments that drift along the boundary between them (20-40 particles per event, velocity 0.3-1.2px/frame, lifespan 120-200 frames, leaving 8-frame trails with exponential opacity decay).

## Animation
PHASE 1 (0-15s, frames 0-900): Cells perform slow pulsing breath—each cell's noise displacement amplitude oscillates between 8px and 25px using sin(frameCount * 0.008 + cellSeed) with individual phase offsets. Cells drift with Brownian motion (0.15-0.4px/frame in random walk, constrained to stay 60px from canvas edges). Interior textures are static. Two cells slowly approach each other in the center zone, closing from 180px to 25px separation over this phase. PHASE 2 (15-35s, frames 900-2100): First cell SPLIT EVENT at frame 900—the two closest cells each subdivide into two smaller cells (original 280px cell becomes two 160px cells) using bezier-curved split line (animation duration: 120 frames, exponential easing). New cells inherit parent texture but with 90° rotation applied. Simultaneously, boundary tendrils activate between all cells within 40px (6-10 tendrils appear, growing from 0 to full length over 45 frames with cubic easing). Cell drift velocity increases to 0.6-1.1px/frame. Pattern migration particles begin emitting (15 particles/second along contested boundaries). PHASE 3 (35s+, frames 2100+): Cells enter MERGE-SPLIT CYCLE—every 8-12 seconds, a random pair of adjacent cells either merge (bezier boundary dissolves inward over 150 frames, textures blend in overlap zone creating moiré interference) or split (if already large enough >220px). Migration particles intensify to 35 particles/second. Cell drift becomes directional (slow diagonal drift at 0.8px/frame toward top-right at 35° angle, noise-modulated). Boundary tendrils now pulse in thickness (1-4px) synchronized to a 3.2-second oscillator. Background color subtly shifts between #EBEBEB and #F0F0F0 on 18-second cycle (barely perceptible breathing).

## Transformation
Individual cells undergo continuous edge deformation—each vertex's noise-displaced position updates every frame, creating perpetual wobble. Scale transformation during split: parent cell shrinks from 1.0 to 0.4 over 40 frames (exponential ease-in), then splits into two children that grow from 0.4 to 0.7 over 80 frames (ease-out). During merge: overlapping cells' boundaries dissolve—the bezier curve separating them shortens its control points inward by 2.1px/frame until cells share a unified perimeter. Texture patterns within merged cells create interference zones (overlapping chevrons create moiré, overlapping halftones create beat patterns) for 90 frames before blending to dominant texture. Pattern migration particles: spawn at full opacity, drift 0.6px/frame tangent to cell boundary, opacity decays exponentially (opacity = pow(0.96, age_in_frames)), trail renders last 8 positions with opacity *= 0.65 per step back.

## Rhythm
Macro rhythm: 18-second breath cycle (subtle background pulse) subdivided into three 6-second phrases. Micro rhythm: cell edge wobble at 0.008 frequency creates 2.1-second oscillation period per vertex (but vertices are phase-offset, so full cell perimeter appears to ripple continuously). Split/merge events occur stochastically every 8-12 seconds (random interval) creating punctuation. Tendril growth is quick (45 frames = 0.75s) but tendril lifespan is long (90-180 frames = 1.5-3s), creating overlapping temporal layers. Particle emission is continuous once activated but with burst intensification during merge events (sudden 2x spike for 60 frames). Overall pacing: contemplative slow-build in Phase 1 (meditative), increasing complexity in Phase 2 (active), steady-state flux in Phase 3 (hypnotic perpetual motion).

## Interaction (mouse)
Mouse X position (mapped 0 to width) controls global noise evolution speed: left edge = 0.0005/frame (nearly frozen), right edge = 0.008/frame (vigorous wobble), center = default 0.002/frame. Mouse Y position (mapped 0 to height) controls cell drift directional bias: top = upward drift bias (+0.4px/frame Y velocity), bottom = downward bias (-0.4px/frame Y velocity), center = neutral Brownian. Mouse click spawns a radial PRESSURE WAVE from click point: all cells within 250px are pushed away with initial velocity inverse proportional to distance (cells at 50px get 4.5px/frame, cells at 200px get 0.8px/frame), velocity decays exponentially over 90 frames. Click also triggers immediate pattern migration burst (80 particles emitted in 0.3s from nearest cell boundary). If mouse is idle for 8+ seconds, system reverts to autonomous mode: noise speed = 0.002/frame, drift = slow top-right diagonal at 0.8px/frame.
