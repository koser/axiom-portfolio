# Negative Space Mitosis
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Project ID:** AG14
**Library:** p5.js

## Concept
A choreographed study of organic division driven by negative space. Soft, bulbous forms — constructed from smoothed vertex paths with noise-driven perturbations — slowly split and merge, creating transient pockets of void that feel as tangible as the mass. Inspired by the moodboard's interplay between heavy black forms and the white voids they carve, this piece treats emptiness as an active participant in the composition, constantly reshaping the relationship between figure and ground.

## Palette
#F8F8F6 (warm white background — the negative space that breathes), #0D0D0D (primary black — dense organic mass), #1A1A1A (secondary — softer black for depth layering), #F2F2EE (off-white accent — rare glowing seams between forms)

## Composition
Initial state (0-18s): Three small forms (120×95px each) positioned in triangular formation: top-center (canvas.width/2, 180px), bottom-left (canvas.width×0.3, canvas.height×0.7), bottom-right (canvas.width×0.7, canvas.height×0.7). Each form constructed from 28 vertices, slowly orbiting around shared centroid at 0.12px/frame. Mid-phase (18-52s): Forms grow radially (expanding 1.8px/frame) while drifting toward canvas perimeter using centrifugal force vectors. As they enlarge, they begin overlapping, triggering fusion events rather than division. Instead of splitting, forms absorb neighbors, creating 1-2 massive aggregated bodies (400-580px diameter) that dominate 65% of canvas. Late phase (52s+): The large aggregated forms undergo slow fragmentation — small 60-90px satellite forms detach from edges at 1.2px/frame, creating a reversal of the growth pattern. Satellites orbit the remaining core mass in elliptical paths (semi-major axis 280-420px, eccentricity 0.3-0.5). By 90s, composition returns to 4-6 medium forms (180-240px) distributed in loose grid pattern across canvas quadrants.

## Elements
BLOB FORMS: Constructed using beginShape()/vertex()/endShape(CLOSE) with 32-56 control points. Each vertex position calculated from base elliptical path (radiusX 90-260px, radiusY 75-220px, random eccentricity 0.15-0.35) displaced by three-layer noise: noise(angle×0.6 + seed, time×0.0015)×45px for primary undulation, noise(angle×1.9 + seed, time×0.004)×18px for mid-frequency ripples, noise(angle×3.2 + seed, time×0.008)×7px for surface granularity. Forms maintain internal properties: mass (proportional to area), velocity vector (vx, vy), angular momentum (rotation speed -0.15 to +0.15 deg/frame), generation depth (starts 0, increments with each fusion event). FUSION TRAILS: When forms merge, a gradient stroke traces the path between centroids — bezier curve with 3 control points, stroke weight 8-2px over 200 frames (easeOutQuart decay), color interpolates from #F2F2EE (90% opacity) at contact to #1A1A1A (20% opacity) at fadeout. Trail leaves afterimage suggesting directional flow of mass transfer. VOID ECHOES: Semi-transparent circular outlines (stroke only, no fill, #1A1A1A at 25% opacity, weight 2px) appear at positions where negative space pockets exceeded 200×200px for 80+ consecutive frames. Echoes spawn at pocket centroid, expand from 80px to 340px diameter over 420 frames (easeOutCubic), fading opacity to 0% over same duration. Maximum 5 echoes visible simultaneously.

## Animation
PHASE 1 — ORBITAL ASSEMBLY (0-18s, frames 0-1080): Three initial forms spawn at frame 0 with staggered scale-in (form 1: frames 0-75, form 2: frames 25-100, form 3: frames 50-125, each using easeOutBack overshoot). Forms orbit shared centroid (calculated as average of all form positions) with orbital radius decreasing from 240px to 160px over first 600 frames (easeInQuad), creating inward spiral. Each form rotates on local axis at 0.22 deg/frame (form 1), 0.18 deg/frame (form 2), 0.14 deg/frame (form 3). Vertex noise animates with time parameter incrementing 0.0015/frame. At frame 840, orbital motion reverses — radius begins expanding 0.3px/frame, preparing for Phase 2 dispersal. PHASE 2 — AGGREGATION SURGE (18-52s, frames 1080-3120): At frame 1080, each form receives outward velocity vector (1.4-2.1px/frame) directed away from shared centroid, randomized ±25 degrees from radial direction. Forms grow continuously: radius increases 1.8px/frame for first 900 frames (1080-1980), then growth rate decays exponentially (rate = 1.8 × pow(0.995, framesSincePhaseStart-900)). Collision detection activates: when two forms' edge distances < 40px for 45+ consecutive frames, fusion initiates. FUSION SEQUENCE (180 frame duration): Fusing forms accelerate toward each other at 2.4px/frame (easeInCubic for first 90 frames), angular momentum averages between forms causing both to rotate toward alignment (up to 3.5 deg/frame), vertex arrays resample to 48 vertices each then interpolate over final 90 frames (easeInOutQuad), resulting form has radius = sqrt(area1 + area2) and mass = mass1 + mass2. By frame 2400, typically 2 large forms remain (420-580px diameter). Between frames 2400-3120, large forms drift slowly (0.4px/frame) with momentum-based physics (velocity × 0.98 damping per frame), occasionally colliding with canvas edges and reflecting at 0.7 coefficient of restitution. PHASE 3 — FRAGMENTATION CYCLE (52s+, frames 3120+): At frame 3120, fragmentation activates for forms with mass > threshold (area > 150000px²). Every 240 frames, each large form spawns 1-2 satellite fragments at random edge positions (angular position random 0-360°). DETACHMENT SEQUENCE (150 frame duration): Fragment begins as 15px protrusion from parent edge, growing to 60-90px final size over 90 frames (easeOutCubic), simultaneously separating from parent at 1.2px/frame. Fragment inherits parent's velocity plus perpendicular ejection vector (1.8px/frame). Detached satellites enter orbital behavior: calculate gravitational-style attraction toward nearest large form (accel = 0.08 × parentMass / distance² px/frame², max 0.4px/frame²), creating elliptical orbits. Parent forms shrink proportionally to fragment mass lost. By frame 4800 (80s), multiple satellites orbit 1-2 remaining core forms. At frame 5400 (90s), orbital attraction inverts to repulsion (same magnitude), scattering satellites to canvas quadrants where they stabilize into loose grid (forms migrate toward quadrant centers using noise-driven waypoint seeking: vx += (targetX - x) × 0.003, vy += (targetY - y) × 0.003). PHASE 4 — EQUILIBRIUM DRIFT (90s+, frames 5400+): Forms maintain quadrant positions with gentle drift (noise-driven velocity ±0.3px/frame), occasional slow rotations (0.05-0.12 deg/frame per form), continuous vertex wobble. Every 600 frames, two random adjacent forms undergo slow approach-and-bounce sequence: accelerate toward each other at 0.6px/frame over 180 frames, contact triggers elastic collision (velocities reflect and scale by 1.4), forms separate. This creates periodic rhythm preventing total stasis.

## Transformation
SPAWN: New forms (satellites detaching, or initial assembly forms) appear with scale 0.2 and opacity 0%, growing to full size over 90 frames using easeOutBack(t) = 1 + 2.70158 × pow(t-1, 3) + 1.70158 × pow(t-1, 2) for overshoot effect. Opacity fades in linearly over same 90 frames. GROWTH: Continuous radial expansion during Phase 2 modifies base radius parameter per frame: radius += growthRate, vertex positions recalculate from new radius. Forms maintain aspect ratio (ellipse eccentricity constant during growth). FUSION BLEND: When two forms merge, vertex arrays resample to matching 48-vertex resolution using angular interpolation. Position lerp over 90 frames: forms accelerate together (easeInCubic) for frames 0-90 of fusion, then vertex positions interpolate (easeInOutQuad) for frames 90-180. During interpolation, colors blend: fillColor = lerpColor(form1.color, form2.color, t), where t = (currentFrame - fusionStartFrame) / 180. Rotation alignment: both forms rotate to match average angle, rotating at min(abs(angleDelta) / 90, 3.5) deg/frame. FRAGMENTATION: Parent form's vertex array segments: identify vertex cluster nearest to detachment angle (±12% of perimeter), those vertices begin moving outward at 1.2px/frame perpendicular to edge normal, creating budding effect. New fragment form constructs separate vertex array (20-28 vertices) from detached cluster, smoothing into independent blob over 60 frames using bezier-interpolated control point relaxation. Parent form's vertex array repairs gap by redistributing remaining vertices evenly across perimeter over 90 frames. SURFACE DYNAMICS: Vertex displacement recalculates each frame using three-layer noise with time parameters: layer1 time += 0.0015/frame (6.67s period), layer2 time += 0.004/frame (2.5s period), layer3 time += 0.008/frame (1.25s period). Each layer uses different angular frequency multipliers (0.6, 1.9, 3.2), creating complex polyrhythmic surface motion. Forms also exhibit subtle breathing: global scale multiplier = 1.0 + sin(frameCount × 0.011 + form.seed) × 0.06, creating independent 9.5-second breath cycles per form.

## Rhythm
MACRO: The piece follows a cinematic arc — slow intimate assembly (18s), explosive growth and fusion (34s), gradual fragmentation and dispersal (38s+), settling into meditative equilibrium. Event density peaks during Phase 2 (multiple fusion events within 10-20 second windows), then decreases through Phase 3 (fragmentation events every 4-6 seconds), finally reaching sparse rhythm in Phase 4 (collision events every 10 seconds). Overall pacing: contemplative opening → dynamic middle → resolving denouement. MICRO: Individual forms breathe on 9.5-second cycles (sin(frameCount × 0.011)). Surface wobble operates on three simultaneous periods: 6.67s (primary), 2.5s (secondary), 1.25s (tertiary), creating continuously shifting interference patterns. Fusion trails fade over 3.33-second duration (200 frames, easeOutQuart curve: opacity = initialOpacity × (1 - pow(t, 4))). Void echoes expand and fade over 7-second duration (420 frames, easeOutCubic). Orbital motion in Phase 3 has natural periods determined by physics (semi-major axis and parent mass determine orbital period via Kepler-inspired simplified model: period ≈ 8-15 seconds for typical satellite orbits). The approach-and-bounce events in Phase 4 create 10-second rhythmic punctuation.

## Interaction (mouse)
MOUSE POSITION (XY): Mouse X position (mapped 0 to canvas.width) controls fragmentation threshold: mouseX/width mapped to range [80000, 220000] replaces default area threshold (150000px²). Moving mouse left triggers earlier/more frequent fragmentation (lower threshold), right delays fragmentation (higher threshold). Mouse Y position (mapped 0 to canvas.height) controls orbital velocity scaling: mouseY/height mapped to range [0.4, 2.2] multiplies satellite orbital speeds. Top of screen (Y=0) → slow dreamy orbits at 0.4× speed. Bottom of screen (Y=height) → rapid chaotic orbits at 2.2× speed. Middle (Y=height/2) → default 1.0× speed. MOUSE PRESS: Each press injects angular momentum into nearest form within 400px radius: form.rotationSpeed += random(-0.25, +0.25) deg/frame, causing temporary spin-up that decays over 180 frames (exponential decay factor 0.985/frame). If no form within range, spawn a new 140-180px form at cursor position with random initial velocity 0.6-1.4px/frame at angle toward canvas center ±45°. MOUSE DRAG: While dragging, create temporary attractive gravity well at cursor position. All forms within 600px radius experience acceleration toward cursor: accel = 0.15 × (1 - distance/600) px/frame² (linear falloff). Closest form within 200px is directly dragged: position lerps toward cursor at 0.18 per frame (creating springy following behavior). PASSIVE FALLBACK: If no mouse interaction detected for 300 frames (5 seconds), fragmentation threshold returns to default 150000px² over 120 frames (easeOutCubic), orbital velocity scaling returns to 1.0× over same duration, and any temporary gravity wells decay to zero influence.
