# Negative Space Mitosis
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Library:** p5.js

## Concept
A choreographed study of organic division driven by negative space. Soft, bulbous forms — constructed from smoothed vertex paths with noise-driven perturbations — slowly split and merge, creating transient pockets of void that feel as tangible as the mass. Inspired by the moodboard's interplay between heavy black forms and the white voids they carve, this piece treats emptiness as an active participant in the composition, constantly reshaping the relationship between figure and ground.

## Palette
#F8F8F6 (warm white background — the negative space that breathes), #0D0D0D (primary black — dense organic mass), #1A1A1A (secondary — softer black for depth layering), #F2F2EE (off-white accent — rare glowing seams between forms)

## Composition
Initial state (0-12s): A single bulbous form occupies the center, roughly 400×320px, asymmetric with one weighted side, constructed from 32 vertices. It slowly breathes and distorts. Mid-phase (12-45s): The form undergoes mitotic division — it elongates horizontally, develops an indentation at its center that deepens over 8 seconds, then splits into two offspring forms that drift apart at 0.8px/frame. Each offspring can divide again based on internal lifetime counters. Late phase (45s+): 4-7 forms coexist, each with independent division cycles. They cluster toward screen edges (left/right thirds), leaving the center increasingly open. Forms that touch merge back together over 6 seconds, creating unpredictable topology. The composition oscillates between dense clustering (80% mass on edges) and sparse distribution (forms scattered, 60% of canvas is void).

## Elements
BLOB FORMS: Constructed using beginShape()/vertex()/endShape(CLOSE) with 24-48 control points. Each vertex position is calculated from a base circular path (radius 80-220px) displaced by layered noise: noise(angle×0.8 + seed, time×0.002) × 35px for primary wobble, plus noise(angle×2.1 + seed, time×0.005) × 12px for surface texture. Forms have soft, undulating edges that never settle into perfect smoothness. Each form stores an internal age counter (frames since creation) and a generation counter (how many divisions from the original). SEAM LINES: When forms divide or merge, a temporary glowing seam appears — a single bezierVertex() curve connecting the points of contact, drawn with off-white stroke, weight 3-1px over 120 frames (exponential decay), creating a brief surgical scar. VOID PARTICLES: Invisible tracker points that measure the largest connected white regions each frame. When a void pocket exceeds 150×150px for 60+ frames, subtle cross-hair lines (1px weight, #1A1A1A, 40% opacity) mark its center for 180 frames, acknowledging the negative space.

## Animation
PHASE 1 — EMERGENCE (0-12s, frames 0-720): Single central form (seed generation 0) breathes using sin(frameCount×0.018)×1.15 as scale multiplier. Vertex noise offsets animate with time parameter incrementing 0.002/frame. Form rotates 0.08 degrees/frame. At frame 480, form begins horizontal stretch (scale.x increases from 1.0 to 1.6 over 180 frames using easeInOutCubic). At frame 660, center indentation begins: vertices in the middle 40% of the perimeter start moving inward at 0.4px/frame. PHASE 2 — DIVISION CASCADE (12-45s, frames 720-2700): At frame 720, form splits into two offspring at the indentation point. Each offspring has generation 1, initial radius 75% of parent, and drifts at 0.8px/frame in opposite directions (±165 degrees from parent's heading, randomized ±15 degrees). Each offspring inherits the parent's wobble seed offset by 100. Division cycle: every form checks at age = 420 + random(120) frames if it should divide. Division criteria: generation < 3 AND not currently merging. Division animation lasts 180 frames (elongation 120 frames, indentation 60 frames). By frame 1800, 4 forms exist. By frame 2400, 6-7 forms exist. Forms drift using noise-driven acceleration: vx += noise(id×0.1, frameCount×0.001)×0.15 - 0.075, vy += noise(id×0.1+50, frameCount×0.001)×0.15 - 0.075, max velocity 1.2px/frame. PHASE 3 — CLUSTER DYNAMICS (45s+, frames 2700+): Forms exhibit edge-seeking behavior: if distance from canvas center > 280px, continue outward drift; if < 280px, subtle repulsion from center adds 0.05px/frame radially outward. When two forms' bounding boxes overlap for 90+ consecutive frames, merge sequence initiates: forms move toward each other's centroids at 1.5px/frame (easeInQuad), contact point generates a seam line, forms blend into single new form over 120 frames with vertex arrays interpolating. Merged form has generation = max(parent generations)+1, preventing infinite growth. At frame 3600 (60s), introduce slow global rotation: entire coordinate system rotates 0.03 degrees/frame around canvas center, making the cluster spiral. Forms occasionally (5% chance per 60 frames when age > 600) emit a micro-division: a small 40px offspring buds off at 2px/frame for 180 frames, then either merges back or becomes independent.

## Transformation
BIRTH: New forms (offspring from division) appear with scale 0.3, growing to full size over 90 frames using easeOutCubic(t) = 1 - pow(1-t, 3). Opacity fades in from 0% to 100% over the same 90 frames, linearly. AGING: Forms subtly darken as they age — fill color interpolates from #0D0D0D at age 0 to #050505 at age 1800 frames, making older forms slightly denser. DIVISION DEFORMATION: During elongation (120 frames), the form's vertex array is multiplied by scaleX: 1.0 → 1.6 (easeInOutCubic), while scaleY: 1.0 → 0.85 (easeInOutCubic), creating a stretched balloon. During indentation (60 frames), center vertices (40% of perimeter arc) move inward toward the centroid using bezier-interpolated control points that create a smooth pinch. MERGE MORPH: Two forms' vertex arrays (resampled to matching 36-vertex resolution) interpolate over 120 frames. Position interpolation: easeInQuad for first 60 frames (accelerating approach), linear for last 60 frames (blending). Rotation during merge: both forms rotate to align their longest axes, rotating at up to 2 degrees/frame. SURFACE WOBBLE: Vertex displacement recalculates every frame using noise(angle×0.8 + seed, frameCount×0.002), creating continuous undulation. The noise time parameter advances at 0.002/frame, resulting in a slow 8.3-second cycle for each noise frequency component.

## Rhythm
MACRO: Slow, biological tempo. The piece unfolds like time-lapse cellular footage. Primary division events occur every 7-12 seconds (420-720 frames), creating a heartbeat of splitting. Merge events are rarer and slower (6-second duration each), acting as moments of reabsorption. The overall pacing accelerates slightly from Phase 1 (single form, meditative stillness) to Phase 2 (division cascade, more events) to Phase 3 (cluster chaos, overlapping divisions/merges). MICRO: Individual forms breathe on a 4.7-second cycle (sin(frameCount×0.018)). Vertex noise wobbles at two frequencies: primary 5-second cycle (0.002/frame), secondary 2.1-second cycle (0.005/frame), creating polyrhythmic surface texture. Seam lines fade over 2-second duration (exponential decay curve: opacity = initialOpacity × pow(0.95, framesSinceCreation)). The subtle global rotation introduced at 60s adds a 200-second period (one full rotation every 12000 frames).

## Interaction (mouse)
MOUSE POSITION (XY): Mouse X position (mapped 0 to canvas width) controls the global noise time scale: mouseX/width mapped to range [0.0005, 0.004] replaces the default 0.002/frame time increment, allowing the user to speed up or slow down surface wobble (far left = slow breathing, far right = rapid vibration). Mouse Y position (mapped 0 to canvas height) controls form attraction/repulsion: mouseY/height mapped to range [-1.5, +1.5] as an acceleration multiplier toward mouse position. Top of screen (Y=0) → forms repelled from cursor at 1.5px/frame² max. Bottom of screen (Y=height) → forms attracted to cursor at 1.5px/frame² max. Middle (Y=height/2) → no cursor influence, default drift behavior. MOUSE CLICK: Each click forces the form closest to cursor (within 300px) to immediately enter division sequence, bypassing age/generation checks. If no form within range, spawn a new generation-0 form at cursor position with random radius 100-160px and random drift velocity 0.3-0.9px/frame at random angle. PASSIVE FALLBACK: If no mouse movement detected for 240 frames (4 seconds), cursor influence factor decays to zero over 120 frames (easeOutQuad), returning to autonomous drift. Noise time scale returns to default 0.002/frame over the same decay period.
