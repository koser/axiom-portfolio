# Reuleaux Morphology
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Library:** p5.js

## Concept
The moodboard shows a stark black and white geometric composition: a circle containing three curved triangular sections that meet at the center, creating a Reuleaux triangle-like form with thick black boundary lines on a light gray background. The artwork brings this to life through slow, continuous morphing — the curved boundaries breathe and flex, the triangular sections rotate subtly around the center point, and the negative space between forms pulses. The rhythm is meditative and precise, like watching a mechanical organism respire.

## Palette
#0a0a0a (dominant black circles and dividing curves), #e8e8e8 (light gray background fill), #1a1a1a (deep black bands top and bottom), #f5f5f5 (brightest white in interior sections), #2a2a2a (mid-tone blacks in boundary transitions)

## Composition
Centered on canvas: a perfect circle approximately 240px diameter occupies the middle of a 600x600px field. The circle is divided internally by three curved boundaries that meet at a central point, creating three bulbous triangular sections. Two horizontal black bands (60px height) frame the top and bottom edges. The composition is perfectly symmetrical with rotational symmetry around the center. When animated, the central meeting point drifts in a 15px radius circle over 40 seconds, causing all three curved boundaries to flex and distort organically while maintaining their connections. The outer circle boundary remains fixed. The background gray field subtly shifts luminance ±3% with a 25-second sine wave.

## Elements
OUTER CIRCLE: 240px diameter ring, stroke weight 3.5px, no fill, constructed with ellipse() at canvas center. CURVED DIVIDERS: Three curves that originate from the outer circle perimeter at 120° intervals and converge at the center. Each curve is a bezierVertex() path with 4 control points, creating a smooth bulge that varies from 0.85 to 1.15 times the radius. The curves have 4px stroke weight. INTERIOR SECTIONS: Three filled regions bounded by the circle and dividers, filled with #e8e8e8 to #f5f5f5 gradient using vertex() loops that trace the curved boundaries. Each section is drawn as a custom shape with beginShape()/endShape(CLOSE), following the outer arc and two divider curves. HORIZONTAL BANDS: Two rectangles 600x60px at y=0 and y=540, filled with #1a1a1a. CONSTRUCTION: The center point is at (300, 300). Three anchor points on the circle at 0°, 120°, 240° serve as curve origins. Bezier control points are calculated using noise(frameCount * 0.002 + offset) to create organic flex, displaced ±8px perpendicular to the ideal radius vector.

## Animation
PHASE 1 (0-15s): The center convergence point begins to drift in a slow circular orbit, 15px radius, completing one rotation every 40 seconds (0.9° per frame at 60fps). The three curved dividers flex to maintain connection to this drifting point, their bezier control points shifting 0.15px per frame with sinusoidal easing (sin(frameCount * 0.015)). The stroke weight of dividers pulses from 3.5px to 4.5px over 8 seconds (sine wave). PHASE 2 (15-45s): The rotation speed of the center point increases to 1.2° per frame. Each of the three interior sections independently rotates around the global center by ±2° over 20 seconds with offset phases (section 1: sin(t), section 2: sin(t + PI*0.66), section 3: sin(t + PI*1.33)). The outer circle stroke weight pulses from 3px to 5px over 12 seconds. Background luminance oscillates ±5% over 18 seconds. PHASE 3 (45s+): All elements enter a synchronized breathing mode. The center point expands its orbit to 22px radius over 10 seconds (exponential ease). The three curved boundaries flex more dramatically, with control points displaced ±12px using Perlin noise (noise scale 0.003). The entire composition scales from 0.95x to 1.05x over 30 seconds with cubic easing, anchored at canvas center. Stroke weights breathe together: all boundaries pulse 3-6px over 15 seconds in unison.

## Transformation
BOUNDARY CURVES: Each curve is defined by 4 bezier control points. Over time, control points drift using 2D Perlin noise with different seeds per point. Displacement magnitude starts at ±4px (0-15s), increases to ±8px (15-45s), and reaches ±12px (45s+). Movement speed is 0.002 noise steps per frame. INTERIOR FILLS: The three sections subtly shift luminance independently. Each section's fill color oscillates between #e8e8e8 and #f5f5f5 using sin(frameCount * 0.008 + sectionOffset) with phase offsets of 0, 2.1, 4.2 radians. Transition duration: 20 seconds per full cycle. CENTER POINT ORBIT: Position calculated as centerX = 300 + cos(angle) * orbitRadius, centerY = 300 + sin(angle) * orbitRadius. Angle increments 0.9-1.5°/frame depending on phase. Orbit radius expands from 15px to 22px over 55 seconds total with smooth exponential easing (easeInOutCubic). SCALE PULSE: Canvas context scaled around (300,300) using scale(scaleFactor) where scaleFactor = 1.0 + 0.05 * sin(frameCount * 0.0015). Full cycle: 30 seconds.

## Rhythm
MACRO RHYTHM: The artwork follows a 90-second macro cycle divided into three 30-second movements. Movement 1 establishes the gentle drift (largo, 40bpm equivalent). Movement 2 introduces complexity and layered oscillations (moderato, 60bpm equivalent). Movement 3 reaches maximum fluidity with synchronized breathing (andante, 50bpm equivalent). After 90 seconds, parameters gradually return to initial state over 15 seconds, then the cycle repeats. MICRO RHYTHM: Individual elements have distinct periodicities creating polyrhythmic texture. Center orbit: 40s. Divider flex: 8s. Stroke pulse: 12s. Section rotation: 20s (with phase offsets). Background luminance: 25s. These periods are chosen to avoid perfect synchronization, creating evolving phase relationships. No element hits exactly on the beat — all timings use irrational multiples to prevent visual clicking.

## Interaction (mouse)
PROXIMITY INFLUENCE: Mouse position creates a repulsion field affecting the three curved dividers. When mouse approaches within 150px of any divider's midpoint, that divider's bezier control points shift away from the cursor by (150 - distance) * 0.15px. The effect falls off with inverse square: displacement = max(0, (150 - dist) / 150)^2 * 25px in the direction opposite the mouse vector. This creates a tactile sensation of pressing into a membrane. CLICK TO RESET: Clicking anywhere triggers a 3-second smooth return animation where all parameters lerp back to their default states (center orbit resets to initial angle, noise offsets reset, scale returns to 1.0). During reset, all elements use easeInOutCubic interpolation. HOVER HIGHLIGHT: When mouse is within the outer circle boundary (distance to center < 120px), the stroke weights of all boundaries increase by 1px and stroke color shifts from #0a0a0a to #2a2a2a over 0.5 seconds (lerp with smoothing). MOUSE VELOCITY: Fast mouse movement (velocity > 8px/frame) causes the interior sections to briefly rotate an additional ±5° in the direction perpendicular to mouse motion, with exponential decay back to baseline over 2 seconds (decay factor 0.95 per frame).
