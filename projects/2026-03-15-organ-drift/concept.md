# Organ Drift
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Library:** p5.js

## Concept
Biomorphic forms orbit a void, splitting and reforming in cycles of cellular mitosis. The piece explores tension between organic growth and geometric constraint — soft blobs collide with hard edges, their collision points becoming new centers of rotation. The emotional register is clinical yet visceral, like watching cell division under time-lapse microscopy.

## Palette
#0d0d0d (deep background — surgical blackness), #17b4a6 (teal — cellular membranes), #f28b9c (pink — vulnerable tissue), #e63946 (red — arterial urgency), #1d2340 (navy — dense interior mass)

## Composition
The canvas is divided into invisible orbital lanes: an inner circle (radius 120px from center), mid ring (radius 220px), and outer band (radius 340px). Phase 1: 8 large blobs orbit the center void in the mid ring, rotating clockwise at different speeds. Phase 2: blobs migrate outward to the outer band while spawning smaller offspring that occupy the now-empty inner circle, creating a dense core. Phase 3: the composition inverts — small elements drift to edges while large forms collapse toward center, leaving the mid ring as negative space. Edge behaviour: elements crossing screen boundaries wrap to opposite side but lose 40% opacity, creating ghosting trails.

## Elements
Primary blobs: 8 irregular organic shapes built from 12-point bezier curves, base diameter 60-90px, stroke weight 0px, filled. Secondary buds: 24 smaller circular forms, diameter 15-25px, semi-transparent (alpha 0.6), same colour set. Collision markers: temporary circles appearing at intersection points, diameter 3px, expanding to 18px over 24 frames then vanishing, white with alpha 0.8 to 0. Orbital trails: thin arcs (stroke weight 0.5px, alpha 0.15) showing the previous 180-frame path of each primary blob, updated every frame, colour matching parent element.

## Animation
Phase 1 (0-18s): 8 primary blobs orbit center clockwise in mid ring at varying speeds — blob 1 at 0.8deg/frame, blob 2 at 1.2deg/frame, blob 3 at 0.6deg/frame, staggered in 45deg increments. Blobs rotate on their own axis counter-clockwise at 0.3deg/frame. Each blob subtly deforms using Perlin noise to shift bezier control points ±4px every 12 frames with linear interpolation. Collision detection: when blob edges overlap (distance < sum of radii), spawn collision marker and both blobs recoil 8px with exponential ease-out over 18 frames. Phase 2 (18-42s): blobs expand diameter by 1px every 4 frames while migrating outward at 1.5px/frame toward outer band. At 24s mark, each blob spawns 3 secondary buds that inherit parent velocity but add radial inward velocity of -2px/frame toward inner circle. Buds oscillate size sinusoidally between 15-25px with 90-frame period, phase-offset by 30 frames per bud. Primary blob orbital speed decreases by 0.02deg/frame every second. Phase 3 (42s onward): secondary buds reach inner circle and begin chaotic drift using noise field — direction changes every 45 frames, speed 0.8-2.2px/frame. Primary blobs in outer band begin gradual inward collapse at 0.5px/frame toward center with cubic ease-in. At 56s, blobs begin mitosis — each splits into two smaller copies (70% original size) that orbit each other at 2.4deg/frame while continuing inward drift. At 72s, all motion pauses for 2 seconds, then resets to Phase 1 configuration with smooth 4-second transition, but colours rotate (teal becomes pink, pink becomes red, red becomes navy, navy becomes teal).

## Transformation
Primary blobs: birth at 0 frames with scale 0, grow to full size over 60 frames with ease-out cubic. During orbit, bezier control points drift using noise(time * 0.01) mapped to ±4px displacement, creating breathing organic distortion. When spawning secondary buds (Phase 2), parent blob flashes to white (alpha 0.4) for 6 frames then returns. During mitosis (Phase 3), blob splits with 30-frame animation — stroke appears (3px white, alpha 0.7) along division axis, blob elongates 150% along perpendicular axis over 18 frames, then separates with 2px/frame outward velocity, stroke fades over 12 frames. Secondary buds: appear instantly at full size, pulse scale 0.9x to 1.1x sinusoidally every 90 frames. Opacity oscillates 0.4 to 0.8 over 120 frames offset by birth time. Collision markers: spawn at 3px diameter, expand linearly to 18px over 24 frames while opacity drops from 0.8 to 0, then removed from array.

## Rhythm
Macro rhythm: 72-second complete cycle with 4-second reset transition. Divided into three unequal movements — meditative Phase 1 (18s), generative Phase 2 (24s), chaotic-then-resolved Phase 3 (30s). Micro rhythm: blobs update position every frame but deformation updates every 12 frames, creating polyrhythmic visual stutter. Collision events are unpredictable bursts (3-8 per 10-second window depending on blob proximity). Mitosis creates sudden 2-second silence at 72s mark — all motion stops, creating dramatic punctuation before restart. The colour rotation every 72s prevents exact repetition, making the piece watchable for 5-6 minutes before patterns become fully predictable.

## Interaction (mouse)
Mouse X position controls global time dilation: at left edge (x=0), animation runs at 0.4x speed; at right edge (x=width), runs at 1.8x speed; center is 1.0x normal speed, mapped with exponential curve for subtle control near center and dramatic control at edges. Mouse Y position controls gravitational center offset: at top (y=0), center shifts upward by 80px; at bottom (y=height), shifts downward 80px; vertical center is default canvas center. This offset affects orbital paths — blobs stretch their elliptical orbits accordingly. Mouse click spawns a temporary repulsion force: all elements within 150px of cursor recoil outward at 4px/frame with exponential decay over 90 frames. Click position spawns a white ring that expands from 0 to 200px diameter over 60 frames, stroke weight 2px fading from alpha 1.0 to 0. If mouse is stationary for 8 seconds, a subtle pulse indicator appears near cursor (12px circle, white, alpha 0.3, pulsing 0.8x to 1.2x scale every 60 frames) to suggest interactivity. Passive fallback: if no mouse movement detected for 15 seconds, system introduces autonomous random gravitational offsets (±60px X/Y) changing every 180 frames with smooth interpolation.
